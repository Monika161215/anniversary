The Story That Changed My Life

Chapter 1

The Day Everything Changed

Tanya was first my student. One day during class she received a phone call and stepped outside.

When she came back, something felt different. Her expression had completely changed.

When I asked if everything was okay, she said yes. But her face told another story.

After insisting gently, she finally told the truth — Grade 3 brain tumor.

The classroom atmosphere suddenly changed. Everyone was shocked. I stopped my lecture, turned off my computer, and focused only on making sure she felt calm and supported.

"Everything will be alright."
Chapter 2

Small Gestures That Meant Everything

After that day something slowly changed between us. The next day she sent me Instagram and Snapchat requests.

Soon she started bringing small surprises for me.

First she brought Shiro, because once in class I had mentioned that I liked it.

A few days later she brought chocolate. Then again she brought thepla for me.

🍫 🍯 🫓

These small gestures slowly created something beautiful between us.

Chapter 3

The Hidden Hints

We started talking more on Instagram. She once mentioned that she wanted to go watch a movie after February 6 during Valentine week, but she had no one to go with.

She also asked me what type of movies I liked.

I said: "Action movies."

But life was already writing our love story.
Chapter 4

The Proposal

Because the numerology of that date matched her Mulank (4)
Chapter 5

The Beginning

On 10 February we went for dinner at Urban Nukkad.

After dinner we attended a music show.

The Strongest Girl I Know

The Get Well Soon Party

Her surgery was scheduled for 7 March 2026. Everyone was worried and tense.

So I planned a surprise. On 5 March, with the help of her classmates and my friends, we organized a Get Well Soon party.

We cut cake. We ate together. We laughed together. For a moment, fear disappeared.

The Emotional Promise

After the party we went outside for photos. But I had another surprise.

In the parking area I kissed her.

Then I made a symbolic promise. I slightly cut my thumb and filled her maang with my blood.

"Even as a husband, I stand with you."

We both cried together. Then she left for the airport. And I left for work.

The Miracle

On 7 March 2026 her surgery was completed.

And the most beautiful news came.

She was completely fine. No memory loss. No complications.

It felt like life gave us another chance.
